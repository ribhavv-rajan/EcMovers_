What to Pack First When
Moving in Windsor-Essex

One of the most common packing mistakes is starting in the wrong place — either packing essentials too early or leaving too much for the last day. Here's the order that experienced movers recommend for Windsor-Essex customers who want a stress-free packing experience.

Start 4–6 Weeks Out: Non-Essential Storage Items

The safest items to pack first are things you definitely won't need before moving day:

  • Seasonal decorations (holiday items, off-season décor)
  • Books and magazines
  • Collectibles, figurines, and display items
  • Artwork and wall hangings
  • Extra linens and towels (keep one set per person out)
  • Items in attic, basement, or storage rooms
  • Guest room contents
  • Hobby and craft supplies you won't use in the coming weeks

3–4 Weeks Out: Rooms You Use Less Often

  • Formal dining room (china, serving dishes, table linens)
  • Living room décor, picture frames, and non-daily-use items
  • Home office (files, books, extra supplies — keep daily work items)
  • Children's toys they don't use daily

💡 Label every box immediately after packing — not at the end of the session. It's easy to forget what went into which box once you're in a packing rhythm.

1–2 Weeks Out: Most of the Home

  • Most kitchen items — keep only what you need for the next two weeks (one pot, one pan, basic utensils, coffee maker)
  • Most clothing — keep a week's worth of clothes accessible
  • Bedroom furniture and bedding — keep one bed set per person out until the last night
  • Bathroom supplies — keep daily essentials out
  • Electronics — keep in use until needed, pack carefully with original boxes if available

Last 2–3 Days: Daily Essentials

  • Daily clothing and toiletries
  • Coffee maker, kettle, and last kitchen basics
  • Phone chargers and daily electronics
  • Medications
  • Pet food, bowls, and supplies
  • Important documents — these should go in your personal bag, not the truck

Don't Pack: Your Essentials Bag

Put together a bag or box that travels with you (not in the moving truck) for the first night:

  • One change of clothes and pyjamas per person
  • Toiletries and medications
  • Toilet paper and hand soap
  • Phone chargers and a power bank
  • Snacks and water bottles
  • Important documents (lease, ID, insurance)
