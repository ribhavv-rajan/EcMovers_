Piano Movers in Windsor:
Safe Moving for Upright and Grand Pianos

Pianos are among the most demanding items to move. An upright piano can weigh 400–800 lbs, a baby grand 500–600 lbs, and a full concert grand well over 1,000 lbs. Beyond weight, pianos are acoustically delicate — dropping or impacting them can damage strings, soundboards, and tuning pins that are expensive to repair. Specialty piano movers in Windsor use the right tools and techniques to move your instrument safely.

Upright Pianos vs. Grand Pianos: What's Different

The moving approach differs significantly based on piano type:

  • Upright pianos — moved on a piano board dolly, kept upright throughout. The main challenges are stairs, tight hallways, and door width.
  • Baby grand and grand pianos — the lid, legs, and pedal lyre must be removed before the body is moved on its side on a skid board. This requires skill to avoid cracking the case or damaging the pedal mechanism.
  • Digital pianos — lighter but still awkward; the stand must be disassembled and the keys protected.

What Piano Movers Bring

  • Piano board dollies and four-wheel skates
  • Heavy-duty straps and moving blankets
  • Skid boards for grand piano transport
  • Ramps for loading into a truck without steps
  • Knowledge of proper lifting technique to avoid instrument damage

💡 After any move, pianos typically need retuning — the vibration of transport affects string tension. Budget for a tuning 2–4 weeks after the move, once the instrument has acclimatized to its new environment.

Preparing for a Piano Move

  • Measure doorways at both origin and destination — most upright pianos are 58–60 inches wide
  • Lock the keyboard lid if possible to prevent keys from shifting
  • Clear a path from the piano to the exit — move rugs and obstacles in advance
  • Let the movers know about any stairs, tight turns, or elevation changes

Does Moving a Piano Void the Warranty?

Some manufacturer warranties require professional moving for coverage to remain valid. Check your piano's documentation before the move.
