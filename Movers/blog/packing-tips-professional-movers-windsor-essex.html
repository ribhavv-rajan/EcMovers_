Packing Tips from Professional
Movers in Windsor-Essex

Packing is where most moves either succeed or fail. Good packing means fewer broken items, faster loading and unloading, and a more organized first day in your new home. Here's what professional movers in Windsor-Essex actually do — and what they wish more clients did before they arrived.

Start With the Right Supplies

  • Small boxes for heavy items: books, canned goods, tools
  • Large boxes for light bulky items: pillows, linens, stuffed animals
  • Dish pack boxes (double-wall) for kitchenware and fragile items
  • Mirror and picture boxes for artwork and large mirrors
  • Packing paper — not newspaper (ink transfers)
  • Bubble wrap for glasses, ornaments, and electronics
  • Quality packing tape — cheap tape fails under weight
  • Permanent markers in two colours for labelling

💡 The single most common packing mistake: boxes that are too light on top and too heavy at the bottom. Fill all air gaps with crumpled packing paper so nothing shifts inside the box.

Room-by-Room Packing Order

Pack in this order to minimize disruption to daily life:

  1. Storage rooms, attic, basement — items you never use
  2. Guest bedroom and rarely used rooms
  3. Living room decorations, books, and non-essentials
  4. Dining room and kitchen (except daily essentials)
  5. Bedrooms (except what you need the last few nights)
  6. Bathroom and kitchen essentials — pack last

Labelling That Actually Helps

  • Write the destination room on the top AND side of every box
  • Use a coloured sticker system — one colour per room — so movers know instantly where each box goes
  • Mark fragile boxes on all four sides and the top
  • Write "OPEN FIRST" on your essentials boxes

Protecting Fragile Items

  • Wrap each glass or dish individually in packing paper
  • Stand plates vertically in boxes — they're stronger on edge than flat
  • Stuff glasses with packing paper before wrapping
  • Use clothing or towels as padding inside boxes for oddly shaped items
  • Never leave empty space in a fragile box
