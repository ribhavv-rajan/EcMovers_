Moving Gym Equipment Safely
in Homes and Commercial Spaces

Gym equipment is some of the heaviest, most awkward, and most expensive-to-damage items in any home or commercial space. Moving it safely requires planning, the right equipment, and — for some pieces — partial disassembly. Here's what you need to know.

Before Moving Day: Prepare Your Equipment

  • Treadmills: Fold the deck if foldable, secure the belt, and remove any safety clips or water bottle holders
  • Ellipticals: Fold the pedal arms if possible and secure any loose components with tape
  • Squat racks and power cages: Disassemble completely — bag and label all bolts by section
  • Cable machines: Remove weight stacks one plate at a time and stack them separately
  • Dumbbells and free weights: Pack in small, sturdy boxes or use dedicated weight bags — never overload
  • Resistance bands and accessories: Group and bag together

💡 Always photograph your equipment setup before disassembly — especially cable machine setups and multi-function stations. Reassembly is much faster with a visual reference.

The Biggest Challenge: Getting Equipment Out

The most common obstacle in gym equipment moves is the exit route. Basement gyms are particularly challenging:

  • Measure all equipment at its widest point before moving day
  • Measure the stairwell width and any landing turns
  • Confirm whether the equipment can be further disassembled if the stairs won't accommodate it
  • Some treadmills and large machines literally cannot exit a basement without full disassembly — know this before the crew arrives

Commercial Gym Moves: Additional Considerations

Commercial gym moves involve volume — many machines, a lot of rubber flooring, mirrors, and sometimes water fountains or sauna units. Additional planning considerations:

  • Schedule the move for early morning or overnight when the gym is closed
  • Have staff pre-clean and prepare equipment the day before
  • Plan the layout of the new space before moving day — it's much faster when movers know exactly where each machine goes
  • Arrange removal of rubber flooring separately from equipment — it's heavy and awkward in rolls
