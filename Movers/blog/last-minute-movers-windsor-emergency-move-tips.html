Last-Minute Movers in Windsor:
Tips for a Smooth Emergency Move

Emergency moves happen. A relationship breakdown, a sudden job offer, an unexpected eviction notice — sometimes you need to move faster than any plan allows. Here's how to stay organized and keep an emergency Windsor move from spiralling into chaos.

Priority 1: Book a Mover Immediately

Before you pack a single box, call moving companies. Good movers fill up fast, and last-minute availability is limited. When you call:

  • Be honest about the timeline — don't say "two days" if you mean "tomorrow"
  • Give your best estimate of room count and large items
  • Ask specifically about same-day or next-day availability
  • Confirm pricing upfront — last-minute moves may carry a premium

💡 Even in an emergency, get a written confirmation of your booking, start time, and price before hanging up. Verbal agreements are too easily forgotten under stress.

Emergency Packing: Triage Your Belongings

When you're packing fast, don't try to be perfect — triage instead:

  • Tier 1 (pack first): Documents, medications, valuables, electronics, essentials for the first week
  • Tier 2 (pack next): Clothes, bedding, kitchen essentials, important personal items
  • Tier 3 (pack last or donate): Décor, books, hobby items, things you can live without

Use garbage bags for soft items like clothes and bedding — faster than boxes and still manageable for movers.

What to Leave Behind — and How

In a genuine emergency move, leaving some items is sometimes necessary. If you can't take everything:

  • Photograph anything you're leaving for insurance or legal purposes
  • Arrange for a second pickup or storage if needed
  • Don't leave items that can't be replaced — documents, medications, irreplaceable sentimental items

After the Move: What to Handle Immediately

  • Notify Canada Post of your address change
  • Update your address with your employer and bank
  • Transfer or cancel utilities at the old address
  • Change your locks at the new address if you have any security concerns
