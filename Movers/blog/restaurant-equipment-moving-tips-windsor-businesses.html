Restaurant Equipment Moving Tips
for Windsor Businesses

Relocating a restaurant is one of the most operationally demanding types of commercial move. Every hour your kitchen is non-operational costs you money, and the equipment involved is heavy, plumbing-connected, and health-regulated. Here are the most practical tips to keep your Windsor restaurant relocation on track.

Tip 1: Plan the Sequence of the Move

Not everything should be moved at once. Plan in phases:

  • Move dry storage, smallwares, and dining room furniture first — nothing requires special disconnection
  • Move refrigeration units after they've been defrosted and emptied (plan 24–48 hours ahead)
  • Move gas appliances last — they require professional disconnection immediately before moving
  • At the destination, set up major equipment (walk-ins, ranges) before smallwares

Tip 2: Book Trades Before You Book Movers

You'll need licensed professionals to handle disconnection and reconnection of:

  • Gas lines — ranges, fryers, salamanders (licensed gas fitter required)
  • Plumbing — dishwashers, prep sinks, ice machines (licensed plumber required)
  • Electrical — walk-in coolers, hood systems (licensed electrician required)

Book these trades first — their availability determines your moving timeline, not the other way around.

💡 Coordinate your trades and movers around the same window. Gas gets disconnected → movers load and transport → gas gets reconnected at the new location. Don't let these happen days apart.

Tip 3: Defrost Refrigeration Early

  • Commercial refrigerators and freezers need 24–48 hours to fully defrost
  • Walk-in coolers need additional time and planning — coordinate with a refrigeration tech
  • Run down perishable inventory in the days before the move
  • Clean all refrigeration units after defrosting — moving is easier when units are dry and clean

Tip 4: Measure Before Anything Is Moved

  • Measure all major equipment at its widest point
  • Measure all doorways and kitchen access points at both locations
  • Confirm that your commercial range, walk-in, or dishwasher will actually fit through the new kitchen entrance

Tip 5: Have Your Health Inspection Lined Up

Many municipalities in Ontario require a health inspection at a new restaurant location before you can reopen. Contact Windsor-Essex County Health Unit in advance to understand the requirements and schedule the inspection as soon as possible after the move.
