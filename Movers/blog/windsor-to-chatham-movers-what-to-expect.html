Windsor to Chatham Movers:
What to Expect

Moving from Windsor to Chatham is a common relocation in southwestern Ontario — whether for work, family, or a change of pace. At roughly 75 km apart via the 401, it's close enough to do in a single day but far enough that the move is priced differently than a purely local one. Here's what to expect.

How Windsor to Chatham Moves Are Priced

Because of the distance involved, Windsor-to-Chatham moves are typically priced in one of two ways:

  • Flat rate: A single price for the entire job, including travel. This gives you certainty before moving day.
  • Hourly + travel: Hourly rate for the moving work, plus a travel charge (usually a flat fee or a per-km rate) for the drive between Windsor and Chatham.

Always get this in writing — specifically ask whether travel time and fuel are included or added on top.

💡 For a Windsor-to-Chatham move, a flat-rate quote is usually easier to budget — you won't be watching the clock for 75 km of highway driving.

How Long Does a Windsor to Chatham Move Take?

Expect the following rough timelines for typical home sizes:

  • 1-bedroom apartment: 4–6 hours total (including drive)
  • 2-bedroom home: 6–8 hours total
  • 3-bedroom home: 8–12 hours total

These vary with packing readiness, access at both locations, and specialty items.

What to Prepare Before Moving Day

  • Have everything packed and labelled before the crew arrives — there's no time to spare on a day-trip move
  • Share the Chatham address and any access notes (gate codes, parking instructions) with your mover in advance
  • Confirm someone will be at the Chatham destination to receive the truck
  • Plan for any specialty items (pianos, hot tubs) early — these require additional crew or equipment

Updating Your Address After the Move

  • Canada Post mail forwarding (canadapost.ca)
  • CRA address update (My Account or by phone)
  • Ontario driver's licence and health card (ServiceOntario)
  • Your bank, employer, and insurance providers
