Apartment Moving Tips
for Windsor Tenants

Moving out of an apartment in Windsor involves more logistics than a typical house move. Shared elevators, limited parking, building rules, and damage deposits all add layers of complexity. Use these tips to make your apartment move as smooth as possible.

Before Moving Day: Talk to Your Building Management

Contact your building superintendent or property manager at least two weeks before your move date to:

  • Book the freight elevator (or confirm which elevator movers should use)
  • Get the allowed moving window — many Windsor buildings restrict moves to specific hours or days
  • Find out if a parking permit is needed for the moving truck
  • Ask about required hallway protection (floor runners, door frame protectors)
  • Confirm the damage deposit process for move-out inspection

💡 Always get elevator bookings in writing from your building manager — verbal confirmations can be forgotten, especially in busy buildings.

Packing Tips for Apartment Moves

  • Use small boxes for heavy items — large boxes of books will be too heavy to carry safely through hallways
  • Label every box with the destination room at your new place, not just contents
  • Pack an essentials box for the first night — don't put it in the truck
  • Wrap furniture legs and corners to protect hallway walls
  • Disassemble large furniture (beds, wardrobes) before moving day

On Moving Day: Apartment-Specific Tips

  • Prop open the apartment door and the stairwell or elevator door safely
  • Have someone manage the elevator if the building doesn't have a hold button
  • Keep pets secured in a carrier or in a room — hallways get busy
  • Do a final sweep of all closets, storage lockers, and the parking spot
  • Take photos of the apartment after you've emptied it for your records

Move-Out Checklist for Windsor Tenants

  • Clean all surfaces, appliances, and bathrooms
  • Patch any small nail holes if required by your lease
  • Return all keys and access fobs
  • Cancel or transfer your utilities, internet, and renter's insurance
  • Update your address with Canada Post, CRA, and your bank
