How to Pack Like a Pro:
Tips That Actually Protect Your Stuff

Bad packing is the number one cause of damage during a move — and most of the damage we see in our years of moving Windsor families is entirely preventable. Here's exactly how our professional crew packs, and how you can do the same.

The Golden Rules of Packing

1. Heavy items in small boxes, light items in large boxes

This is the single most important rule. Books, dishes, and tools should always go in small boxes — a full large box of books is nearly impossible to lift safely and will bow the bottom. Light items like pillows, blankets, and lampshades can go in large boxes without issue.

2. Never leave empty space in a box

Empty space allows items to shift during transit and is the primary cause of breakage. Fill gaps with packing paper, bubble wrap, clothing, or even towels. Every box should be firm when you press the top — not spongy.

3. Label every single box

Label the top and at least two sides of each box with: destination room, brief contents, and FRAGILE if applicable. Use colour-coded stickers per room — it makes unloading dramatically faster and helps our crew know where every box goes.

💡 Our crew uses a simple colour code: red for kitchen, blue for master bedroom, green for living room, yellow for bathroom. Pick up coloured dot stickers at any dollar store.

Packing Room by Room

Kitchen

The kitchen is the most time-consuming room to pack. Start 2–3 weeks before your move. Here's how:

  • Wrap each plate individually in packing paper and stack them vertically (like records) — never flat. Flat stacking multiplies the weight on lower plates.
  • Wrap glasses and mugs individually, place upside-down, and fill the box cell-by-cell using dividers if possible.
  • Pots and pans can nest without wrapping, but put a sheet of paper between each to prevent scratches.
  • Pack sharp knives in folded cardboard sleeves before boxing them.
  • Appliances should go back in their original boxes if you have them. Otherwise, wrap in moving blankets and pack snugly.

Bedroom and Clothing

  • Use wardrobe boxes for hanging clothes — they keep garments on hangers and wrinkle-free.
  • Drawers can often be left packed if the furniture is being moved whole — tape the drawers shut with masking tape.
  • Use suitcases for heavy folded clothing — they're sturdy, have wheels, and get moved anyway.
  • Disassemble bed frames and label every bolt and screw in a zip-lock bag taped to the headboard.

Fragile Items and Art

  • Mirrors and framed artwork should be wrapped in moving blankets and stored vertically in the truck, never flat.
  • Electronics should be packed in their original boxes when possible. If not, wrap in anti-static bubble wrap and pack snugly.
  • Lamps: remove shades and bulbs. Pack shades in a large box with paper fill. Wrap lamp bases in blankets.

Bathroom

Pack the bathroom last — you'll need it until the very end. Use zip-lock bags for toiletries that could leak. Place inside a box lined with a garbage bag as an extra layer of protection.

What NOT to Pack

  • Flammable items: paint, propane, lighter fluid — movers legally cannot transport these
  • Perishable food that won't survive the move
  • Plants (many movers won't take them on long-distance moves)
  • Important documents — keep these with you in your car
  • Valuables (jewellery, cash, passports) — always travel with you
