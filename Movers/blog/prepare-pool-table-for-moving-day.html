How to Prepare a Pool Table
for Moving Day

Pool table moving is almost entirely handled by the professionals — but there are a few things you can do before they arrive to make the process faster, safer, and more efficient. Here's your preparation checklist.

What You Need to Do Before the Crew Arrives

1. Clear the Room Around the Table

Pool tables are large and require significant working space on all sides. Before moving day:

  • Remove all items from on and around the table — balls, cues, chalk, covers, racks
  • Move any nearby furniture (barstools, chairs, side tables) completely out of the room
  • Roll up any rugs in the room
  • Clear the pathway from the pool room to the exit

2. Measure the Access Route

  • Measure every doorway on the path from the pool room to the truck
  • Measure stairwell width if the table needs to go up or down stairs
  • Note any tight corners or turns in the route
  • Share all measurements with your mover when booking

💡 Pool table slate sections travel flat — the truck needs to be parked where a long flat route exists from the door to the loading area. Check for steps, curbs, or elevation changes.

3. Know Your Table Type

Tell your mover in advance:

  • Is it a slate table or non-slate (MDF)?
  • How many slate pieces — one, two, or three?
  • What size is the table — 7-foot, 8-foot, or 9-foot?
  • Is the felt being replaced or reused?

This information determines the crew size, equipment needed, and time required.

What the Movers Handle

Everything from here is the mover's responsibility: pocket removal, rail disassembly, felt removal, slate lifting, frame disassembly, transport, reassembly, levelling, and felt installation. You don't need to touch any of it — doing so without the right knowledge can cause damage.
