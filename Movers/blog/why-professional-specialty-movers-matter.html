Why Professional Specialty Movers
Matter for Heavy Items

It's tempting to think any moving company can handle any item. After all, moving is moving — right? Not quite. Specialty items require specific knowledge, equipment, and technique that standard residential movers simply don't have. Here's why it matters and what the consequences of getting it wrong can be.

What Counts as a Specialty Item?

  • Pianos (upright, baby grand, grand)
  • Hot tubs and spas
  • Pool and billiard tables
  • Safes and gun safes
  • Gym and fitness equipment
  • Commercial kitchen equipment
  • Antiques and high-value artwork
  • Large aquariums
  • Industrial and warehouse machinery

What Can Go Wrong Without the Right Movers

Moving a specialty item with an unqualified crew is a risk that can result in:

  • Injury — hot tubs and pianos can weigh 500–1,000 lbs; improper lifting technique puts movers and bystanders at risk
  • Damage to the item — a cracked piano soundboard, a cracked slate pool table section, or a hot tub cabinet that's been cracked from improper support can cost thousands to repair or replace
  • Damage to the property — scratched hardwood floors, gouged doorframes, damaged staircase railings
  • Insurance complications — many moving company policies don't cover specialty items moved without declared specialist capability

💡 Always ask specifically: "Do you have experience moving [this item] and do you carry the specialist equipment for it?" A reputable mover will answer confidently and in detail.

What Specialist Equipment Looks Like

  • Piano board dollies and skid boards (not just standard appliance dollies)
  • Four-wheel furniture skates rated for 500+ lbs
  • Appliance dollies with reinforced stair-climbing capability
  • Ramps rated for the load weight
  • Heavy-duty moving straps and harnesses
  • Specialist crating where required for very high-value items

The Cost of Getting It Wrong

Retuning a piano after a bad move: $150–$300. Replacing cracked pool table slate: $300–$600 per section. Repairing a hot tub cabinet damaged during an amateur move: $500–$2,000+. The cost savings of hiring a cheaper, unqualified mover are almost never worth the risk on these items.
