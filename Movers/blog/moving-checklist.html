The Ultimate Moving Checklist:
8 Weeks Before to Moving Day

Moving is one of the most logistically complex things most people ever do — and the number one reason it goes wrong is a lack of planning. We've helped thousands of Windsor-Essex families move, and the ones who have the smoothest experience are the ones who start planning early.

Here's the checklist we wish every client had before calling us.

8 Weeks Before Moving Day

Decide on your moving company

Don't leave this to the last minute, especially if you're moving in summer (May–August), when most reputable movers book up weeks in advance. Research companies, read Google reviews, and get 2–3 quotes. Make sure your mover is licensed and insured — ask for proof.

Start decluttering

The less you move, the less it costs — and the faster the process. Go room by room with three boxes: Keep, Donate, Discard. Be ruthless. You'll be glad you did on moving day.

Notify important parties

  • Your landlord (if renting) — check your notice period in your lease
  • Your bank, insurance, and financial institutions
  • Canada Post — set up mail forwarding at canadapost.ca
  • CRA — update your address for tax purposes
  • Your employer's HR department

6 Weeks Before

Confirm your mover

Once you've chosen your moving company, sign the contract and pay the deposit to lock in your date. For EC Movers Ltd, a small deposit secures your booking — no surprises.

Start collecting packing supplies

  • Boxes in multiple sizes (small for heavy items, large for light items)
  • Packing tape and a tape gun
  • Bubble wrap and packing paper
  • Permanent markers for labelling
  • Coloured stickers for room-coding boxes

Begin packing non-essentials

Start with items you won't need for weeks: seasonal decor, books, extra linens, guest room items, and anything in storage. Label every box with its contents and destination room.

4 Weeks Before

Transfer utilities and services

  • Electricity, gas, and water — schedule transfer or cancellation
  • Internet and TV — schedule installation at new address
  • Phone — update your address with your provider
  • Health cards, driver's licence, and vehicle registration

Confirm elevator or parking bookings

If you live in an apartment or condo, contact your building management now to book the elevator and a parking spot for the moving truck. Buildings often have limited windows and book up fast.

2 Weeks Before

Pack most of your home

Leave out only what you'll genuinely need for the next two weeks: daily clothing, toiletries, kitchen essentials, electronics, and bedding. Pack everything else.

Confirm your move details

Call your moving company to confirm the date, time, crew size, truck size, and any special requirements (piano, large furniture, tight stairwells). Share any access information they'll need.

Prepare an essentials box

Pack a separate, clearly labelled box (or bag) that travels with you — not in the truck — with:

  • Phone chargers and important electronics
  • Medications
  • One change of clothes per person
  • Snacks and drinks for moving day
  • Important documents (ID, lease/deed, insurance)
  • Toilet paper, soap, and a towel for the first night

Moving Day

Before the movers arrive

  • Disassemble any large furniture (beds, desks) if you haven't already
  • Defrost the refrigerator the night before
  • Ensure clear pathways from each room to the exit
  • Protect floors and doorframes in high-traffic areas

During the move

  • Do a walkthrough with the crew before they start loading
  • Point out fragile or high-value items that need extra care
  • Keep children and pets safely away from the loading area
  • Stay available to answer questions — don't disappear

Before you leave

  • Do a final walkthrough of every room, closet, cabinet, and outdoor space
  • Check attic, basement, storage lockers, and garage
  • Hand over keys to your landlord or new owner
  • Take meter readings for electricity and gas
  • Sign off on the inventory with your moving company

After You Move In

  • Test all locks, windows, smoke detectors, and appliances
  • Locate your electrical panel, water shutoff, and gas shutoff
  • Update your address with any remaining services
  • Leave a review for your moving company — it helps them and future customers
