How Far in Advance Should You
Book Movers in Windsor?

One of the most common mistakes Windsor residents make when planning a move is leaving the booking too late. The best moving companies fill up weeks in advance — especially during peak season. Here's a practical guide to how far ahead you should book based on your specific situation.

Local Moves in Windsor: How Far in Advance?

  • Off-peak (September–April): 1–2 weeks' notice is usually sufficient, but 2–3 weeks gives you date flexibility
  • Shoulder season (May and late August): 3–4 weeks recommended — things are picking up
  • Peak season (June–August): 4–6 weeks minimum — many reputable Windsor movers book up completely on popular summer dates
  • Month-end: Add an extra week regardless of season — the last few days of any month are always busier

💡 If your move date is fixed (closing date, lease start), book your mover the same day you confirm that date — don't wait a week.

Specialty Item Moves: Book Even Earlier

Pianos, hot tubs, and pool tables require specialist crew and equipment that not all movers have available every day:

  • Piano moves: book 3–4 weeks out minimum, 6 weeks in summer
  • Hot tub moves: same — 3–4 weeks minimum, earlier in summer
  • Pool table moves: 2–3 weeks minimum

Specialty moves also require pre-planning conversations with the mover — about access, disconnection, and preparation — that can't happen the day before.

Long-Distance Moves from Windsor: Book the Earliest

For moves to Toronto, Ottawa, Sarnia, London, or cross-country:

  • Book 6–8 weeks in advance
  • Long-distance pricing requires a proper assessment — don't expect an accurate quote over the phone without a full inventory
  • The more notice you give, the more likely you are to get your preferred date and a competitive price

What Happens If You Book Too Late?

  • Your preferred date may be unavailable from reputable companies
  • You may be forced to choose a less experienced mover or pay a last-minute premium
  • Less pre-move communication time — details get missed when booking is rushed
  • Higher stress for everyone involved

A Simple Rule

As soon as you know your move date, book your movers. There's almost no scenario where booking earlier causes a problem, but booking too late causes plenty.
