What to Expect When Hiring
Piano Movers in Windsor

Moving a piano is unlike moving any other piece of furniture. It's heavy, acoustically sensitive, and the wrong move (literally) can cause damage that costs thousands to repair. Here's exactly what to expect from start to finish when you hire piano movers in Windsor.

The First Call: What Information to Have Ready

When you contact a piano moving company, have the following information ready:

  • Piano type — upright, console, baby grand, grand, or digital
  • Piano brand and approximate age if known
  • Origin address and destination address
  • Floor level at both locations (ground floor, second floor, basement)
  • Whether there are stairs — and how many
  • Any tight hallways, sharp corners, or narrow doorways on the route

What Happens on Moving Day

A professional piano moving crew will typically:

  • Arrive and assess the piano and the full access route before touching anything
  • For uprights: place the piano on a piano board dolly and navigate carefully to the truck
  • For grands: remove the lid, legs, and pedal lyre; position the body on a skid board; load on its side
  • Secure the piano in the truck with moving straps — it should not shift at all in transit
  • Unload and position at the destination, reassembling grand piano components

💡 Professional piano movers will never rush the job. If the route requires additional time — a tight landing, a narrow hallway turn — that time is spent carefully, not hurried.

Stairs: The Most Common Challenge

Stairs are the most common complication in Windsor piano moves. For uprights on stairs, the piano is tilted and moved on a piano board by two to four people depending on the weight. For grands on stairs, a mechanical stair-climbing device may be used. Always disclose stairs when requesting a quote — the price and crew size change accordingly.

After the Move: What to Expect

  • Your piano will likely need retuning 2–4 weeks after the move
  • Allow the piano to acclimatize to its new environment before tuning
  • Avoid placing the piano near exterior walls, heating vents, or windows initially
