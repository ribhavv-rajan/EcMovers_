How to Protect Furniture
During a Move in Windsor

The most common source of damage during a move isn't dropping items — it's rubbing, scraping, and shifting in transit. Professional movers in Windsor use a combination of wrapping, padding, and strategic loading to keep your furniture arriving in the same condition it left. Here's what those methods look like and how you can prepare.

Moving Blankets: The Foundation of Furniture Protection

Heavy quilted moving blankets are wrapped around every major piece of furniture — sofas, dressers, tables, headboards, and appliances. They protect against scratches from contact with walls, doorframes, and other items in the truck. Professional movers carry dozens of blankets on every job. Never let a mover load unwrapped furniture into a truck.

Shrink Wrap: Securing the Blankets

Plastic stretch wrap (shrink wrap) is used to hold moving blankets in place and protect fabric furniture from dirt and moisture. It's especially useful for:

  • Upholstered sofas and chairs (prevents fabric from catching on edges)
  • Mattresses (wrapped to keep them clean during transport)
  • Securing drawers in dressers so they don't slide open during loading
  • Bundling sets of chair legs or table parts together

💡 Never wrap wood furniture directly in plastic shrink wrap — it can trap moisture and damage finishes. Always apply a moving blanket first, then wrap over it.

Disassembly: Often the Safest Option

Large furniture pieces — beds, dining tables, wardrobes — are often safer transported in pieces than as a whole. Disassembly reduces the risk of damage through doorways and makes the pieces manageable. Always bag hardware in labelled zip-lock bags and tape them to the furniture piece they belong to.

Smart Loading in the Truck

How items are loaded into the truck matters enormously. Professional movers load:

  • Heaviest items against the cab wall (appliances, dressers, wardrobes)
  • Fragile and lighter items toward the back and on top
  • Padding between items that are in contact with each other
  • Straps to secure the load against shifting during transit

What You Can Do to Help

  • Empty all drawers before moving day — lighter furniture is easier to manoeuvre and less likely to tip
  • Disassemble what you can the night before
  • Remove legs from tables and coffee tables if they unscrew easily
  • Wrap mirrors and artwork in bubble wrap and place in mirror boxes
